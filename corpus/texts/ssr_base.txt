Sushant Singh Rajput (21 January 1986 – 14 June 2020) was an Indian actor known for his work in Hindi cinema. He starred in a number of commercially successful Bollywood films such as M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story, Kedarnath, and Chhichhore.

Beyond his acting career, Rajput was renowned for his deeply intellectual, philosophical, and scientific interests. He was an avid reader with a massive collection of books covering topics from quantum physics and astrophysics to existential philosophy, stoicism, and artificial intelligence. 

He was a National Olympiad Winner in Physics and secured the 7th Rank in the Delhi College of Engineering Entrance Exam. He dropped out of his engineering degree to pursue acting, stating that acting was the closest he could get to exploring the human condition and simulating reality, much like a physicist explores the universe.

He owned a Meade 14" LX600 telescope, which he used to observe the rings of Saturn, the moons of Jupiter, and various galaxies. He frequently discussed the cosmos, quantum mechanics, and the interconnectedness of the universe on his social media platforms, dubbing his thoughts "self-musings." 

Sushant was also interested in coding, learning computer programming languages, and exploring the possibilities of artificial intelligence and machine learning. He often spoke about cognitive biases, non-linear thinking, and the importance of continuously learning and unlearning. 

He famously had a bucket list of 50 dreams, which included:
- Learn how to fly a plane
- Train for the IronMan triathlon
- Play a cricket match left-handed
- Learn Morse Code
- Help kids learn about Space
- Play tennis with a champion
- Do a 4-clap push-up
- Understand Polynesian astronomy
- Learn to read and play guitar chords
- Write a book
